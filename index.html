What is the ‘Ashtalakshmi Darshan’ Youth Exchange Programme?

AU
Assam University Media Cell 28 March 2026

The Ashtalakshmi Darshan is a 14-day youth exchange programme designed to connect 1,280 students from 28 non-Northeastern states with the eight northeastern states (“Ashtalakshmi”) of India. Funded by the Ministry of Development of North Eastern Region (MDoNER), it promotes cultural understanding, national integration, and experiential learning through visits, academic sessions, and community interactions.

Objective: To foster “Ek Bharat Shreshth Bharat” by allowing young delegates to explore the heritage, ecology, and development of the Northeast.

Target Audience: 1,280 students across 32 batches from non-NER states.

Structure: 14-day immersive visits featuring workshops, cultural evenings, yoga, and academic modules at premier institutions like Rajiv Gandhi University (Arunachal Pradesh) and IIT Guwahati.

Participating States: The programme pairs northeastern states with others, focusing on exchange and unity (e.g., Goa/Uttarakhand with Arunachal Pradesh, Tamil Nadu/Chandigarh with Sikkim).

Duration: The programme began in late 2025 and runs through 2026, targeting to complete 32 batches.

The initiative aims to strengthen the bonds between the youth of different regions and enhance understanding of the Northeast’s diversity.
